Aurora

An aurora, also known as the northern or southern lights, is a natural light display predominantly seen in polar regions around the Arctic and Antarctic. It is caused by the interaction of charged particles from the sun with atoms in Earth's atmosphere, resulting in vibrant and shifting curtains of light that can appear in various colors such as green, red, blue, and purple.
